  • The Douglas Lemma for von Neumann Algebras and Some Applications, submitted (16 pg.)
    [arxiv:1707.04378] Abstract

    Abstract: In this article, we discuss the well-known Douglas lemma on the relationship between majorization and factorization of operators, in the context of von Neumann algebras. We give a proof of the Douglas lemma for von Neumann algebras which is essential for some of our applications. We discuss several applications of the Douglas lemma and prove some new results about left (or, one-sided) ideals of von Neumann algebras. A result by Loebl-Paulsen characterizes C∗-convex subsets of B(H), the set of bounded operators on a complex Hilbert space H, as those subsets which contain C∗-segments generated from operators within that subset. We define the notion of pseudo C∗-convexity for a subset of a Hilbert C∗-bimodule over a C∗-algebra with the aspiration of it being a practical technical tool in establishing C∗-convexity of a subset. For a von Neumann algebra R, we prove the equivalence of the notions of C∗-convexity and pseudo C∗-convexity in Hilbert R-bimodules. This generalizes the aforementioned Loebl-Paulsen result which may be formulated in a straightforward manner in the setting of C∗-convexity in Hilbert B(H)-bimodules.



  • A Bootstrapped Proof of the Minkowski Determinant Inequality for Operators on a Hilbert Space, preprint available upon request.
    Abstract

    Abstract: For positive operators A, B in a finite von Neumann algebra R with a faithful normal tracial state τ and the associated Fuglede-Kadison determinant Δ, we give a new proof of the inequality, Δ(A+B) ≥ Δ(A)+Δ(B). Further if A is regular, we conclude that equality holds iff B is a non-negative scalar multiple of A. The novelty of the argument lies in first establishing the weaker inequality Δ(A+B) ≥ 2√Δ(A) √Δ(B) using a generalized version of the Hadamard determinant inequality, and bootstrapping it to prove the required inequality. For positive-semidefinite n × n complex matrices A, B, this gives us the Minkowski determinant inequality, det(A+B)1/n ≥ det(A)1/n + det(B)1/n.
